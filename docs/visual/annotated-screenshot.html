Legend

Cell States (A-D, I)

A — Orange Covered Cells Cells the player hasn't acted on yet. Could be filled (mined) or empty. The player must deduce which. Marking a filled cell turns it blue. Opening an empty cell reveals it as dark. Incorrectly opening a filled cell triggers a mistake.
B — Blue Filled Cells (Marked) Cells the player has correctly identified as filled/mined. Blue is the "solved-positive" state. Some blue cells contain a number — see "I — Flower" below.
I — Flower (Blue Cell With Number) A blue (filled) cell containing a clue number. The number counts filled cells within a 2-hex radius (the 18 cells in the two concentric rings around it — not just the 6 immediate neighbors). For example, the "8" flower in the lower-right means 8 of those 18 surrounding cells are filled. Flowers can also use curly-brace and dash modifiers for contiguity.
C — Dark Open Empty Cells Revealed empty cells. Very dark fill, barely above background color. May contain clue numbers indicating how many of the cell's six hex neighbors are filled. Clue numbers can be plain (3), curly-braced ({3}), or dashed (-3-) — see Number Types below. Some cells show ? instead, meaning no clue is available.
D — Missing Hexes Hex positions absent from the puzzle. Shown as faint grey outlines or completely absent. Not interactive. They shape the grid's irregular silhouette and affect neighbor/line counts for adjacent cells.

HUD Elements (F-H)

F — REMAINING Counter Blue rounded-rectangle badge showing filled cells left to find. Shows "71" in this screenshot. Primary progress indicator.
G — MISTAKES Counter Red/coral badge tracking errors. Shows "0" here. The only alarm-colored element in the entire game.
H — MENU Vertical text at upper-left, below macOS window controls. Very low visual weight to keep focus on the puzzle.

Number Types (E + in-cell)

E — Line Clues (Column/Diagonal) Numbers at the end of a column or diagonal, indicating the total count of filled cells along that line. Appear along the top edge (column clues, e.g., "6", "3", "4"), along the left and right sides (diagonal clues), and also inside the grid in missing hex positions — where they count filled cells along a partial line from that position to the grid edge. Can be plain, curly-braced, or dashed.
Plain Number (3) A bare number inside an open cell or at a line endpoint. For cells: how many of six neighbors are filled. For lines: total filled count. No contiguity information — filled cells could be in any arrangement. Visible throughout the dark cell corridors (region C).
Curly-Brace ({n}) — Contiguous All filled cells form a single connected group — no gaps. For neighbor clues: an unbroken arc around the cell. For line clues: a single run along the line. A stronger constraint than a plain number.
Dashed (-n-) — Discontiguous Filled cells are not all in one group. At least one gap separates them into two or more clusters. Subsets within a cluster can be adjacent. Visible on cells showing e.g. -3- or -4- in the dark corridors.

Game Mechanics

Objective

Identify all filled (mined) cells on the hex grid using logical deduction from clue numbers. Mark filled cells (they turn blue). Open empty cells (they reveal their dark interior and possibly a clue number).

Mistakes

Two actions trigger a mistake: opening a cell that is actually filled (mined), or marking a cell as filled when it is actually empty. The goal is zero mistakes.

Neighbor Clues (Open Empty Cells)

A number inside an open empty cell tells you how many of its six immediate hex neighbors are filled. Modified by {} (contiguous) or - - (discontiguous) notation.

Flower Clues (Blue Filled Cells)

A number inside a blue (filled) cell is a "flower." It counts filled cells within a 2-hex radius — the 18 cells in the two concentric rings around it, not just the 6 immediate neighbors. Same contiguity modifiers apply.

Line Clues (Column/Diagonal)

Numbers at the end of a column or diagonal indicate the total filled count along that line. Appear at the top edge, left/right sides, and also inside the grid in missing hex positions (counting a partial line from that point to the grid edge). Same contiguity modifiers apply.

Contiguity: {n}

Curly braces guarantee all filled cells form one connected group — no gaps in the ring of neighbors or along the line.

Discontiguity: -n-

Dashes guarantee the filled cells are split into 2+ separate groups. At least one gap must exist between them.

Grid Shape

Irregular organic boundary. Some hex positions are missing entirely (grey outlines). Missing hexes aren't interactive but affect neighbor counts and line clue totals.

Hex Orientation

Flat-top hexagons. Flat edges on top and bottom; vertices point left and right. Offset-column stagger.
